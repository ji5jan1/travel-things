# NYU MS in Publishing

I spent my first two years in New York in the NYU MS in Publishing program.

You may wonder, why, Kelly, you already have a MA, why bother to go for another master's degree?

I decided to switch path for a reason. In Hong Kong, English majors are expected to become teachers after they graduate. It's a designated path that I was trying to refuse. So, I thought, how can I apply my literary knowledge into practice? The first thing I thought about was publishing, to work with books. I had no idea what the publishing industry is about, but I thought it might be a good idea if I want to make money with words.

In the best possible case, I want to be a writer. I haven’t written something as long as a novel, or a full collection of poetry, but I have always dreamed of doing so. I still remember trying to draft a novel on the Chinese writing grid paper in elementary school. Though after 20 years, I still haven’t met a story that I want to create a whole novel about. I still write from time to time, and most of the time short prose or some poetry, because I know well that the habit of writing needs to be built up. I cannot just wake up someday and suddenly write a complete novel—impossible unless I’m enlightened by God.

Back to the MS program. I would say what I learned about the real world in these two years have elevated all the education that I received in my life. In the past, I almost lived in the ivory tower—being “bookish theoric”. But in this program, I finally feel that I am a useful person, because I can now code a website, read financial statements, and I have created a business proposal from scratch! Now that I feel more connected to the real world, I am less anxious when I think about my career. I don’t think I’d ever give up my writer’s dream, but at least for now, I am able to secure a job and take care of all the bills myself.

Since I haven’t experienced the publishing industry, I wasn’t sure what I am capable of, and what aspect interests me. Unlike my classmates that have specific interests, I made myself experience a bit of everything. From how to negotiate a book deal as a literary agent, to creating social media marketing campaigns, I have seen many facets of this industry. Now I feel like I can be everything, I have so much more career options than being a teacher in my hometown!

In the classroom, I learned to become a publisher. Outside the classroom, I learned to take care of myself.

It was the first time I ever signed a lease agreement, filed taxes (that’s a requirement for all international students, no matter whether we work), assembled furniture, and cooked a full meal for myself. Because of some personal past events, I decided to spend most of my time in solitude. Luckily, my mental health improved a lot in the sun of Central Park. Though HRs may be disappointed seeing that I didn’t use this time for an internship or join some clubs. But yeah, I have my reasons to keep a low profile.

Now that I have graduated, I’m ready to become a responsible(?) adult and pay my own bills. TBH, I started to write this to share my experiences in the program, and some courses that I loved, but it turned out like this. But I guess this is also a form of experience sharing? At least I enjoyed typing this out!
