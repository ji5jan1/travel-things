# Barnes & Noble Union Square 
📍 Address: 33 E 17th St, New York, NY 10003 <br>
December 24, 2025

What else can you say about this store? It's no doubt the best bookstore in NYC!

Though there're a lot of great indie bookstoresm like the Strand, you have to admit that B&N has the biggest storefront. They sell everything, from children's books to CDs, and even a space for events. I luckily participated in a talk for NYU Publishing students in November, 2024. I enjoyed the talk on how people started their career in the publishing industry and it was truly inspiring.

![B&N * NYU Talk Presentation](https://github.com/kelly990918/travel-things/blob/main/book-observations/usa/bn-unionsq/67C9F5ED-84FA-43DE-879C-DD957BDA944C_1_105_c.jpeg)

I hate to admit that, but I love their children's books corner. Compared to the boring children's book corner in the [local library](https://maps.app.goo.gl/Hrp7u5LsQKYMD2uT7) I visited as a child, the children's book corner look so engaging and it makes me feel so young and happy just watching the decorations. Though I'm not a consumer for children's books, every time I visit the store, I would visit the Harry Potter corner, and appreciate the great art in all the special editions. I told myself, "When I have my own house, I'm going to collect them all." 

From my observation, the most popular brands are the Dr. Seuss (PRH), Dog Man (Scholastic), and of course, our beloved Percy Jackson (Disney Hyperion). But I guess the general public won't realize the difference in between publishing houses, not to mention being a fan of a specific publishing house. I guess this is why publishers would always put the brand/ series/ authors first when creating brand awareness, because selling is more about resonating with the consumers than chasing after them.
